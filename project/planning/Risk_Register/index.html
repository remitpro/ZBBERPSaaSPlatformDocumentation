    ZBB ERP Module Implementation¶

    Document Version: 1.0
    Last Updated: February 10, 2026
    Owner: Project Manager

    Risk Scoring Matrix¶

    Probability: Low (1), Medium (2), High (3)
    Impact: Low (2), Medium (4), High (6)
    Risk Score: Probability × Impact

    Risk Levels: - Critical (12+): Immediate action required - High (9-11): Active monitoring and mitigation - Medium (6-8): Regular monitoring - Low (≤5): Periodic review

    Detailed Risk Analysis¶

    R-001: GL Integration Complexity¶

    Description: Integration with General Ledger may be more complex than anticipated due to legacy system constraints, API limitations, or data format incompatibilities.

    Impact if Materialized: - Project delays of 2-4 weeks - Additional development effort required - Potential scope reduction - Budget impact: $50K-$100K

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Proof of Concept (PoC) - Conduct GL integration PoC in Month 1, Week 2-3 2. Dedicated Resource - Assign experienced integration specialist full-time 3. Vendor Support - Engage ERP vendor technical support early 4. Fallback Plan - Design manual data upload process as temporary workaround

    Monitoring: - Weekly integration status reviews - PoC completion by Month 1, Week 3 - Escalate to steering committee if PoC fails

    Status: Active - PoC scheduled for Month 1

    R-002: User Resistance to Change¶

    Description: Business users may resist transition from familiar Excel-based process to new ERP system, leading to low adoption and continued shadow processes.

    Impact if Materialized: - Low user adoption (<70%) - Continued use of Excel (shadow IT) - Failed project objectives - Wasted investment

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Executive Sponsorship - Secure visible, active support from CFO and executive team 2. Change Champions Network - Identify 15-20 champions (1-2 per business unit) by Month 2 3. Early Involvement - Include key users in requirements validation and design (Month 1-2) 4. Comprehensive Training - Multi-tiered training program starting Month 8 5. Benefits Communication - Monthly newsletter highlighting time savings and benefits 6. Quick Wins - Demonstrate early value through pilot with willing department

    Monitoring: - User satisfaction surveys (quarterly) - Training attendance tracking - System usage analytics post go-live - Change champion feedback sessions (monthly)

    Status: Active - Change management plan in development

    R-003: Scope Creep¶

    Description: Stakeholders may request additional features or changes beyond agreed scope, leading to timeline delays and budget overruns.

    Impact if Materialized: - Timeline delays of 1-3 months - Budget overrun of 10-20% - Team burnout - Delayed benefits realization

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Formal Change Control Process - Implement change request form with impact analysis 2. Steering Committee Approval - All scope changes require steering committee approval 3. Impact Assessment - Mandatory assessment of timeline, budget, resource impact 4. Phase 2 Backlog - Defer non-critical requests to post-implementation backlog 5. Clear Requirements - Detailed FRD with sign-off (completed)

    Change Request Process: 1. Requestor submits change request form 2. PM conducts impact analysis (timeline, budget, resources) 3. Steering committee reviews and approves/rejects 4. If approved, update project plan and communicate

    Monitoring: - Track all change requests in change log - Monthly scope review in steering committee - Baseline vs. current scope comparison

    Status: Active - Change control process to be established Month 1

    R-004: Key Resources Unavailable¶

    Description: Critical team members may become unavailable due to illness, resignation, competing priorities, or reassignment.

    Impact if Materialized: - Project delays of 1-4 weeks per resource - Knowledge loss - Quality issues - Team morale impact

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Cross-Training - Ensure at least 2 people familiar with each critical area 2. Documentation - Comprehensive documentation of all design decisions and code 3. Backup Resources - Identify backup resources for each critical role 4. Knowledge Transfer - Regular knowledge sharing sessions 5. Resource Commitment - Secure written commitment from department heads

    Critical Roles Requiring Backup: - Technical Lead - Functional Lead - Integration Specialists - Lead Business Analyst

    Monitoring: - Monthly resource availability review - Track resource utilization - Early warning system for potential departures

    Status: Active - Backup resource identification in progress

    R-005: Data Quality Issues in CoA Mapping¶

    Description: Chart of Accounts data may have inconsistencies, duplicates, or inactive accounts that complicate mapping and integration.

    Impact if Materialized: - Delays in Phase 1 (2-3 weeks) - Rework of budget templates - Data cleansing effort required - Potential errors in budget consolidation

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Data Quality Assessment - Conduct CoA data quality review in Month 2, Week 1 2. Data Cleansing - Allocate 1 week for data cleansing if issues found 3. Validation Workshops - Conduct validation sessions with Finance team 4. Master Data Governance - Establish CoA freeze during project 5. Reconciliation Process - Build reconciliation checks into system

    Monitoring: - Data quality metrics (duplicates, inactive accounts, missing descriptions) - Weekly data quality reviews during Month 2

    Status: Active - Data quality assessment scheduled for Month 2

    R-006: Performance Issues with Large Datasets¶

    Description: System may experience performance degradation when handling large volumes of budget data, especially during consolidation and reporting.

    Impact if Materialized: - User dissatisfaction - Missed performance SLAs (<3 sec page load) - Potential need for infrastructure upgrade - Additional optimization effort

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Performance Testing - Comprehensive load testing in Month 11 (500 concurrent users) 2. Scalable Architecture - Design for scalability from the start 3. Database Optimization - Proper indexing, query optimization 4. Caching Strategy - Implement caching for frequently accessed data 5. Infrastructure Sizing - Right-size infrastructure based on load projections

    Performance Targets: - Page load time: <3 seconds - Report generation: <30 seconds - Consolidation: <5 minutes for 50 business units - Concurrent users: 500 without degradation

    Monitoring: - Performance testing in Month 11 - Monitor response times during UAT - Capacity planning review

    Status: Active - Will be addressed in Phase 4

    R-007: Regulatory Requirements Change¶

    Description: Banking regulations may change during implementation, requiring modifications to KPI calculations, provisioning logic, or reporting.

    Impact if Materialized: - Scope changes - Rework of KPI engine or financial statements - Timeline delays of 2-4 weeks - Additional testing required

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Flexible Design - Build configurable rules engine for regulatory calculations 2. Regulatory Monitoring - Assign Compliance team to monitor regulatory changes 3. Quarterly Reviews - Review regulatory landscape quarterly with Compliance 4. Configurable Parameters - Make regulatory ratios and thresholds configurable 5. Change Absorption - Include buffer in timeline for potential changes

    Monitoring: - Quarterly regulatory review meetings - Compliance team alerts - Industry association updates

    Status: Active - Quarterly review scheduled

    R-008: Vendor Delays or Issues¶

    Description: ERP vendor may experience delays in providing support, delivering software updates, or resolving critical issues.

    Impact if Materialized: - Project delays of 1-4 weeks - Workarounds required - Quality compromises - Additional costs

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Clear SLAs - Establish service level agreements with vendor 2. Escalation Process - Define escalation path for vendor issues 3. Alternative Vendor - Identify alternative vendor as backup (if feasible) 4. Regular Vendor Reviews - Monthly vendor performance reviews 5. Contractual Penalties - Include penalties for vendor delays in contract

    Vendor SLAs: - Critical issues: 4-hour response, 24-hour resolution - High priority: 1-day response, 3-day resolution - Medium priority: 3-day response, 1-week resolution

    Monitoring: - Track vendor response and resolution times - Monthly vendor performance scorecard - Escalate persistent issues to steering committee

    Status: Active - Vendor contract review in progress

    R-009: Budget Overrun¶

    Description: Project costs may exceed approved budget due to scope changes, resource needs, or unforeseen issues.

    Impact if Materialized: - Budget overrun of 10-20% ($360K-$720K) - Need for additional funding approval - Potential scope reduction - Project credibility impact

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Monthly Budget Tracking - Track actuals vs. budget monthly 2. 15% Contingency - $348K contingency buffer included 3. Early Warning System - Alert if projected variance >5% 4. Cost Control - Strict approval process for unbudgeted expenses 5. Scope Management - Prevent scope creep (see R-003)

    Budget Monitoring: - Monthly budget vs. actual reports - Forecast to completion analysis - Variance analysis and trend tracking

    Status: Active - Monthly budget tracking to begin Month 1

    R-010: UAT Reveals Major Gaps¶

    Description: User Acceptance Testing may uncover significant gaps in functionality or usability that require substantial rework.

    Impact if Materialized: - Go-live delays of 4-8 weeks - Team morale impact - Rework effort and costs - Stakeholder confidence loss

    Mitigation Strategy: 1. Early User Involvement - Include users in requirements and design phases 2. Iterative Demos - Monthly demos to stakeholders starting Month 4 3. Prototype Validation - Validate prototypes with users before full development 4. Phased UAT - Conduct UAT by module, not all at once 5. Clear Acceptance Criteria - Define acceptance criteria upfront for each feature

    UAT Approach: - Module-level UAT during development (Months 6, 9) - Comprehensive UAT in Month 11-12 - >95% test case pass rate required for go-live

    Monitoring: - Track user feedback from demos - UAT defect trends - User satisfaction during UAT

    Status: Active - User involvement plan in development
